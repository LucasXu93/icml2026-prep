# Papers – ICML 2026 Prep

## Evaluation & Benchmarking

**Rethinking Benchmark Saturation in Large Language Models**
*Vandermeer, C., Osei-Bonsu, K., Liang, H. (2026)*
Good framing on eval design — the saturation argument is one we keep
running into internally. Directly relevant to what we're trying to
figure out at TriadVortex.

**Distributional Shift as a Reliability Indicator in Deployed ML Systems**
*Farouqi, N., Castellano, R. (2025)*
Flagged by someone on the team — worth a look before the reliability
workshop. The taxonomy in section 3 is useful even if the rest is dense.

**Beyond Accuracy: A Framework for Production-Readiness Evaluation**
*Johanssen, M., Park, S., Oduya, T. (2026)*
This is the one I keep coming back to. Disagree with some of the
conclusions around latency trade-offs but the methodology is worth
understanding properly.

## Failure Modes & Predictability

**Characterising Silent Failures in Autoregressive Systems**
*Chen, W., Abramowitz, D., Gupta, R. (2025)*
Useful. The distinction between silent and recoverable failures is
something we don't have clean language for yet internally.

**Failure Predictability Under Distribution Mismatch: An Empirical Study**
*Nkemdirim, A., Solberg, E. (2026)*
Solid empirical work — not flashy but the kind of thing that actually
matters in production. Adding to the shortlist.

## Product & Applied ML

**From Research to Deployment: Closing the Capability-Usability Gap**
*Hartley, P., Imai, Y., Ferreira, L. (2026)*
Almost exactly the problem we're working on. Worth seeing if any of
the authors are presenting — the gap framing in the intro is very close
to how I'd describe TriadVortex's core challenge.

**Evaluation Debt: How Deferred Assessment Compounds in ML Pipelines**
*Osei-Bonsu, K., Whitmore, J. (2026)*
The "eval debt" framing is new and I think it'll stick. Flagged for
the team as well — good shared vocabulary to have before Seoul.

**Observability Primitives for Production ML: A Practitioner's Taxonomy**
*Subramaniam, V., Brecht, A., Park, S. (2025)*
Recommended by someone I'm meeting in Seoul. Haven't finished it yet
but the first two sections are strong.
